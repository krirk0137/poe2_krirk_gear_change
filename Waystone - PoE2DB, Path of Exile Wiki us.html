Waystones Item /16
Waystones
Waystones are items that can be used to travel to Maps on the Atlas.

Higher Waystone tiers open higher level areas containing more difficult monsters which drop higher level items and allows the use of higher tier Atlas Passives.

Waystones can be modified to increase the difficulty and reward of monsters encountered in Maps.

Waystones
Waystones are items that can be used to travel to Maps on the Atlas.

Higher Waystone tiers open higher level areas containing more difficult monsters which drop higher level items and allows the use of higher tier Atlas Passives.

Waystones can be modified to increase the difficulty and reward of monsters encountered in Maps.

Revival Availability
Most endgame content allows for a limited number of deaths before you can no longer access the area. Most areas allow unlimited leaving and re-entry for other reasons except while fighting a boss.

On death during a boss fight, the Map Owner may respawn all players into a new instance of the area to attempt the boss fight again.

The number of modifiers on a Waystone will reduce the number of Revivals available allowed when opening a Map.
Powerful Map Boss
Powerful Map Bosses are Map Bosses that are even more difficult and drop even better rewards.

Powerful Map Bosses frequently drop Waystones one Tier higher.

Map Node
This represents a Map. A Waystone can be used to access it.

If you fail to complete the Map, it may be attempted again but will not contain additional content, Essences, Shrines, or Strongboxes. It will not be Corrupted. Tablets cannot be used on failed Maps.

To complete a Map, defeat the Boss.
Cartographer's Strongbox
Cartographer's Strongboxes drop Waystones.
Waystone Tier Progression
Only the Final Powerful Map Boss in a Map Area has a chance to drop a Waystone of a higher tier than the tier of that Map Area.

The chance diminishes the higher the tier of the Waystone used to the create the Map Area, but can be increased again by adding modifiers to Waystones, additional modifiers on Waystones can give increased chance for Waystones to drop in the Map Areas they create.
Map Objective Waystone Drops
The final Rare Monster slain in a Map Area has a chance to drop a Waystone equal to the tier of the Waystone used to create that Map Area.

The chance diminishes the tier of the Waystone used to the create the Map Area, but can be increased again by adding modifiers to Waystones, additional modifiers on Waystones can give increased chance for Waystones to drop in the Map Areas they create.
Zantipi's Medallion

Landmark Area

Waystone

It's infinite and expands in all directions. Now, we're not going to spoil the plot too much here, but corruption from The Beast has blighted the land, and your objective is to help fight it.

In the center of the Atlas you will find the The Ziggurat Refuge, a temple in which the Vaal research the fundamentals of space and time. Here we can open portals to nearby locations and begin cleansing corruption and finding resources.

In order to power the portal device, we will need waystones. Each waystone has a tier which will determine the level of the monsters that you will face.

To go to an area, simply click one that is adjacent to an area you've already completed, insert the waystone and click traverse. Portals will open, allowing you and your party to travel to the area you have selected.

All the areas in endgame feel distinct from locations in the campaign, and we've dialed the randomization up to 11.

The monster packs you will face in each map are totally random and the combination of them can lead to some very interesting combat situations.

There are over 400 monster types in Early Access, and they have a lot of varied and interesting abilities. Depending on which combination you face, you might find some serious challenges.

In order to cleanse the corruption from an area, you will need to defeat all the powerful, rare, and unique enemies in it.

Once you've done that, the area will be marked as completed and you'll be able to travel past it to progress deeper into the world.

The Ezomyte Megaliths

Explore the wild Ezomyte Forests to find mysterious rune-carved stones. Activating the stones will summon a dangerous boss. Once you have defeated it, summon another.. And another.. And another.. And another! You have ten total waves of boss fights which get incrementally more difficult.

The Silent Cave

Enter a crystalline cavern to find a powerful map Boss, surrounded by Rare monsters imprisoned in essence crystals. By freeing and defeating the Rare Monsters, the essence they held will be upgraded to a Greater essence and be added to the imprisoned Map Boss.

How brave are you? How many essences will you add to the boss? Overdo it and you might lose everything, underdo it and you might regret it! It's your choice.

The Fractured Lake

Located in a surreal waterscape, this area contains fractured mirror-like objects. In the Fractured Lake all the Rare monsters are mirrored so you're always fighting two of the same Rare at the same time.

If you manage to overcome that challenge and make it to the end of the Map you'll be presented with a broken Mirror that contains either an Amulet or a Ring that can be crafted to have additional prefixes, or suffixes, at the cost of the other.
